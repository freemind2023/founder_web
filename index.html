The Story Behind the Mission

He Didn't Just Teach.
He Changed the System.

Sanmit Shah started like any ambitious student — following the prescribed path, collecting certificates, hoping the degree would open doors. It didn't. Not the way it should have.

19 years into his career, he kept meeting the same story in different faces: brilliant students, good marks, zero readiness for the real world. Theory without practice. Knowledge without application. Potential without direction.

That empathy became a mission. He didn't just write about change — he built an entirely new education system. Practical EduSkills was born not from a boardroom strategy, but from deep frustration and an equally deep belief: every student deserves a fair shot at a successful career.

Today, with 2000+ transformed students and a movement that grows every morning at 5 AM, Sanmit Shah is not just an educator — he is a revolution in education.

The Hard Truth

Why Degrees Alone
Are Not Enough

The education system was built for a world that no longer exists. Here's what's really happening to graduates today.

01

Pure Theory, Zero Practice

Textbooks describe accounting. They don't simulate a real GST filing. Students graduate without ever touching the tools they'll use every single day.

02

Zero Industry Exposure

College faculty haven't worked in industry for decades. Students learn from people who've never faced a real client, a real deadline, or a real business problem.

03

The Unemployment Gap

India produces millions of graduates yearly. Employers can't find job-ready candidates. The gap between a degree and a career has never been wider.
